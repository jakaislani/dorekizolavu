What You’ll Need to Make Tortilla Roll Ups

These pinwheel sandwiches are super simple to make and you only need a handful of ingredients to pull them together:

  • tortillas or wraps – I used a combination of wraps and burrito size tortillas for these pinwheels. You can of course use smaller tortillas if you prefer, your pinwheels will just be smaller
  • cream cheese – make sure the cream cheese is adequately softened so you can really mix in the cranberries and chives
  • dried cranberries – the cranberries add an unexpected sweetness to these wraps that is so delicious, don’t leave them out!
  • chives or green onions – I used chives because they are both mellower and smaller but either will work.
  • turkey slices – oven roasted turkey works great here and if you can’t find deli meat, leftover turkey, thinly sliced would be delicious
  • Havarti cheese – I chose Havarti because it’s a semi-soft cheese that is mellow and pliable. You don’t want a hard cheese here UNLESS you shred the cheese. The tortillas won’t roll nicely if you have big chunks of hard cheese in there.
  • Romaine lettuce – spinach or butter lettuce could be substituted

How To Make Pinwheel Sandwiches

Tortilla roll ups are incredibly easy to make and these turkey pinwheels are no exception. Let’s do this!

  • Combine softened cream cheese, chopped chives, and dried cranberries in a small bowl. 
  • Spread an even layer of the cream cheese mixture over two tortillas placed side by side, slightly overlapping.
  • Layer on turkey, cheese, and lettuce.
  • Starting at the short end, roll tortillas up tightly and chill for 1 hour before serving.
  • When ready to serve, slice into one inch pieces and place on a plate or serving platter.

    • Variations To Try

    The possibilities are truly endless when it comes to pinwheels. Here are some of our favorite variations:

    • Swapping out turkey for another deli meat is a quick way to change up this easy appetizer. Try ham, roast beef, or salami.
    • Any cheese will work in this recipe but be sure to pair up the cheese with a complementary protein. For example: salami with provolone, roast beef with cheddar or Swiss, ham with cheddar or Swiss. If you use a hard cheese, instead of using cheese slices, grate the cheese and incorporate it into the cream cheese mixture.
    • Instead of Romaine try spinach, butter lettuce, or arugula.

    Tips for the BEST Pinwheels

    • Don’t forget to soften the cream cheese. It has to be soft so you can spread it easily and mix in the chives and cranberries.
    • Spread the cream cheese mixture evenly and generously. If the cranberries are all one side, move them around.
    • Use fresh tortillas or wraps that are nice and soft so they don’t crack or split when you roll them.
    • Roll the tortillas as tightly as possible. This helps keep the pinwheels tight when they are sliced.
    • Use toothpicks to keep them from unrolling.

    Turkey Cranberry Pinwheels

    These Turkey Cranberry Pinwheels are the perfect appetizer! Deliciously creamy with a hint of sweetness, this simple tortilla roll ups recipe is sure to be a hit with friends and family!

    Course Appetizer Cuisine American Keyword pinwheels Prep Time 10 minutes minutes Servings 8 Calories 305kcal Author Trish - Mom On Timeout

    Ingredients

    • 4 tortillas burrito size or wrap size
    • 8 ounces cream cheese softened
    • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
    • 3 tablespoons chopped chives or green onions
    • 12 ounces turkey slices
    • 8 ounces Havarti cheese slices
    • Romaine lettuce ribs removed

    Instructions

    • In a small bowl, combine softened cream cheese, chopped chives, and dried cranberries. Mix until combined.

    • Spread an even layer of the cream cheese mixture over two tortillas placed side by side, slightly overlapping.

    • Layer on turkey, cheese, and lettuce.

    • Starting at the short end, roll tortillas tightly and place in the fridge for 1 hour to set up. If chilling for longer than one hour, wrap tightly in plastic wrap before placing in the refrigerator.

    • When ready to serve, slice into one inch pieces and place on a plate or serving platter.

    • Leftovers can be stored, tightly wrapped, in the fridge for up to 2 days.
