Sienna Rosie Diana Miller is an American-English actress. She was born in New York City and grew up in London. She established her career as a photography model and appeared on the pages of Italian Vogue and posing topless for the 2003 Pirelli Calendar.

Later, she became famous for her major acting breakthrough released in the year 2004 films ‘Layer Cake’ and ‘Alfie’.

Career

Just after making her breakthrough, Sienna Miller started appearing as Edie Sedgwick in Factory Girl (2006) and author Caitlin Macnamara in The Edge of Love (2008), for which she was nominated for the 2008 BAFTA Rising Star Award.

The actress also acted as the Baroness in G.I. Joe: The Rise of Cobra’ which was released in the year 2009. She then subsequently played several roles and got praised by many.

She then rose to fame after appearing as Tippi Hedren in the television film named ‘The Girl’ (2012) for which she earned a nomination for the BAFTA Television Award for Best Actress and the Golden Globe Award for Best Actress- Miniseries or Telefilm.

Moreover, the actress, Sienna Miller became critically acclaimed during the 2010s with her fantabulous performances in films including ‘Foxcatcher’ (2014), ‘American Sniper’(2014), Mississippi Grind (2015), The Lost City of Z (2016), Live by Night (2016), and American Woman (2018), as well as the miniseries The Loudest Voice (2019) for which she gained much praise from the audience.

Achievements

Sienna Miller is the winner of many awards and nominations including ‘BAFTA Television Award, Golden Glove Award for Best Actress- Miniseries or Telefilm, and many others are on the list.
