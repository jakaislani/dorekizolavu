David Walliams Wife: Is David Walliams Married? – David Edward Williams, better known by his stage name David Walliams, is an English comedian, actor, writer, and television personality.

His work with Matt Lucas on the BBC sketch comedy shows Little Britain and Come Fly With Me is what made him famous.

Walliams was a judge on ITV1’s Britain’s Got Talent from 2012 until 2022, when he stepped down. He also writes children’s books, which have sold over 37 million copies worldwide.

He was born at Wimbledon, Merton, London’s St. Teresa’s Maternity Hospital. Walliams made an appearance in the 1999 Mark Gatiss-penned Doctor Who audio play Phantasmagoria from Big Finish Productions.

In the 2005 music video for Charlotte Hatherley’s single “Bastardo,” Walliams, Simon Pegg, Lucy Davis, and Lauren Laverne all made appearances.

David Walliams: My Life with James Bond, a James Bond documentary, was broadcast later that year. He successfully made a comedic television comeback in 2007’s Capturing Mary, where he played a slick and cunning manipulator.

In the Rather You Than Me television film produced by BBC Four, he played comedian Frankie Howerd. Alongside Paul Rudd and Steve Carell, Walliams starred in the comedy Dinner for Schmucks in 2010.

In the sixth season of Doctor Who’s episode “The God Complex,” which aired on BBC One in 2011, Walliams portrayed the odd, mole-like alien Gibbis.

David Walliams made an appearance in the episode “David Walliams: The Genius of Dahl” of the ITV program Perspectives in April 2012. He narrated Are You Having a Laugh? as well in 2012. Top Dog Model on ITV2 and TV and Disability on BBC Two.

Alongside Amanda Holden, Alesha Dixon, and Simon Cowell, Walliams served as a judge on the ITV1 talent competition Britain’s Got Talent from 2012 to 2022. He received recognition for his work on the series at the National Television Awards in 2015, 2018 and 2019, each year as Best Judge. He and Simon Cowell’s on-air chemistry has frequently received praise.

After a scandal over leaked criticisms of contestants made by Walliams erupted in November 2022, it was reported that Walliams had decided to end his ten-year tenure and that he wanted to leave the program on a high note.

In addition to writing for his sketch shows and novel adaptations, he co-wrote the animated film Shadows with filmmaker Edgar Wright for DreamWorks Animation. It came out in 2019.

Walliams also appeared in the British Airways Safety video in 2018, which was produced in collaboration with Comic Relief for the airline’s in-house charity. The lifejacket instruction is Walliams’ most memorable moment in the video. The video has since been reworked with a mixture of the previous version; however, Walliams remains in the new version.
