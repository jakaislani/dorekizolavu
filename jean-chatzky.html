Career

Chatzky is an American journalist, author, and motivational speaker. She is the financial editor of NBC’s TODAY show and AARP’s personal finance ambassador.

Chatzky began her career in the year 1986 as an editorial assistant at Working Woman magazine and rose to become the assistant editor. In the year 1989, she left journalism and joined the equity research department of Dean Witter Reynolds.

Two years later, she returned to journalism as a reporter/researcher at Forbes. She moved to the Dow Jones/Hearst start-up SmartMoney in the year 1992, where she rose from staff writer to senior editor.

After a five-year run, Chatzky joined Money Magazine in 1998. Chatzky has authored several books on personal finance, including “Money Rules: The Simple Path to Lifelong Security” and “Women with Money: The Judgment-Free Guide to Creating the Joyful, Less Stressed, Purposeful (and, Yes, Rich) Life You Deserve”. She has also been featured in various media outlets, including The New York Times, The Wall Street Journal, and Forbes.

Jean Chatzky Awards and Recognition

Chatzky received the Clarion Award for magazine columns from the Association of Women in Communications in 2002, and her radio show received a Gracie Award from the American Women in Radio and Television.

She has also been nominated twice as part of a three-person writing team each time for National Magazine Awards in Personal Service and was named one of the country’s 30 best magazine columnists by the Chicago Tribune in 2003.

In 2009, the Consumer Federation of America awarded Chatzky the Betty Furness Consumer Media Service Award for her nearly two decades of pioneering personal finance education.
