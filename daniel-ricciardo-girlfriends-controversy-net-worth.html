Daniel Ricciardo: Career

Daniel was just a teenager when he started his career. It was 2005 when he entered the Western Australia Formula Ford Championship with his 15-year-old Van Demien.

While being unsuccessful in his first few races, he eventually won the Formula BMW Asian Championship with Eurasia Motors. This became the first of his many victories.

In 2007 Ricciardo switched to the Formula Renault category. By the end of next year, he took home the second trophy in the Western European Cup.

Daniel also became the first Australian to win a British Formula 3 title after David Brabham in 1989. Daniel also won the race in a similar Volkswagen-engined car.

In an invitation-only game by British Racing Driver’s Club, Daniel won the BRDC The Bruce Mclaren Trophy in 2013.

In 2014 he won the Trofeo Lorenzo Bandini. And in the same year, he won the title of Confartigianoto Motodriver of the Year.

He was also named the GQ: Australia sportsman of the year in 2014. Then he went on to consequently win the BRDC The Bruce Mclaren Trophy twice in a row.

Daniel was the victor of BRDC The Innes Ireland Trophy two times in a row in 2014 and 2015.

He was also given the award of Laureus World Sports Award for breakthrough performance of the year in 2015. The next year he won the BRDC The Bruce Mclaren trophy again.

In recent years he won the title of Confartigianoto Motori Driver of the year in 2018.
